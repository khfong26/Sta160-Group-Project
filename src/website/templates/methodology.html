Methodology

1. Data Collection

Our dataset is built by aggregating job postings from Indeed.com. We utilized the JobSpy library to scrape real-time job listings. Additionally, we implemented custom Selenium scripts to handle dynamic content and ensure a comprehensive dataset. The raw data includes job titles, companies, locations, salaries, and full job descriptions.

2. Data Processing & Cleaning

Raw data was processed using Pandas to handle missing values, normalize salary formats, and standardize location names. We employed spaCy for Natural Language Processing (NLP) to extract key skills and technologies from unstructured job descriptions. This allowed us to create a structured "skills" column for every job posting, enabling precise filtering and analysis.

3. Recommendation Model

The core of our "Resume Match" feature is a content-based recommendation model built with scikit-learn. It matches your resume to job postings using a weighted scoring algorithm:

  • TF-IDF Vectorization (55% weight): We convert both your resume and job descriptions (Title + Skills + Description) into numerical vectors using Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency and compare them using cosine similarity. This captures the textual similarity between your profile and the job.
  • Skill Overlap (35% weight): We calculate the percentage of required job skills that are present in your resume. This ensures you have the specific technical competencies requested.
  • Experience Bonus (10% weight): We extract years of experience from both texts and apply a bonus score if your experience level closely matches the job requirements.

Final Score = (0.55 × TF-IDF) + (0.35 × Skill Score) + (0.10 × Experience Bonus)
